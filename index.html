About Satara

The City of Seventeen Peaks

Satara, a historic city nestled in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra, derives its name from the Marathi words "Saat" (seven) and "Tara" (stars or peaks), meaning "seventeen peaks." The city is surrounded by seventeen hills, which gave it this distinctive name.

Historical Significance

In ancient times, Satara was known as "Jaoli" or "Javali". The region gained prominence during the Maratha Empire when it served as the seat of the Chhatrapati (Maratha king) after the fall of Raigad. In 1699, Chhatrapati Rajaram Maharaj made Satara his capital, establishing it as a significant political and cultural center.

The Legend

According to local folklore, the name also has mythological connections. It is believed that the seventeen peaks represent the seventeen stars of the constellation, making Satara a place blessed by celestial powers. The most prominent of these peaks is Ajinkyatara Fort, which stands as a sentinel overlooking the city.

Modern Satara

Today, Satara is known for its rich heritage, natural beauty, and agricultural prosperity. The city continues to honor its glorious past while embracing modernity, making it a perfect blend of history and progress.

The Seventeen Peaks of Satara

Satara is uniquely surrounded by seventeen majestic hills and peaks, which gave the city its distinctive name. The word "Satara" comes from the Marathi words "Saat" (seven) and "Tara" (stars/peaks), literally meaning "seventeen peaks."

Notable Peaks Include:

  • Ajinkyatara Fort - The most prominent peak overlooking the city at 3,300 feet
  • Yawateshwar - Sacred hill with ancient temple
  • Sajjangad - Fort of the good people, home to Sant Ramdas Samadhi
  • Chandan-Vandan - Twin peaks with historical significance

These peaks not only provide natural beauty but also served as strategic defense points during the Maratha Empire. The hills create a natural fortress around Satara, making it an ideal location for the capital.

Geographical Significance: The seventeen peaks form a protective ring around the city, creating a unique microclimate and contributing to the region's agricultural prosperity.

Capital Established in 1699

In 1699, Chhatrapati Rajaram Maharaj, the younger son of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, established Satara as the capital of the Maratha Empire after the fall of Raigad Fort to the Mughals.

Historical Timeline:

  • 1699 - Rajaram Maharaj makes Satara the Maratha capital
  • 1700 - Death of Rajaram; his widow Tarabai continues the struggle
  • 1708-1714 - Satara serves as the seat of Maratha power
  • 1818 - British establish the Princely State of Satara
  • 1848 - Annexed by British after the death of Raja Pratapsinh

Why Satara?

Satara was chosen as the capital due to its:

  • Strategic location surrounded by protective hills
  • Proximity to important forts and trade routes
  • Natural defenses against enemy attacks
  • Fertile land and water resources

Legacy: Though the capital later moved to other cities, Satara remained an important political and cultural center, preserving the rich heritage of the Maratha Empire.

Western Ghats Location

Satara is nestled in the heart of the Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the world's eight "hottest hotspots" of biological diversity.

Geographical Details:

  • Elevation: Approximately 2,300 feet (700 meters) above sea level
  • Coordinates: 17.69°N latitude, 74.01°E longitude
  • Region: Western Maharashtra, India
  • Climate: Moderate with cool winters and pleasant summers

Western Ghats Significance:

The Western Ghats, also known as Sahyadri in Marathi, is a mountain range that runs parallel to the western coast of India. This region is known for:

  • Biodiversity: Home to thousands of endemic species of flora and fauna
  • Water Source: Origin of major rivers including Krishna and Godavari
  • Climate Regulation: Influences monsoon patterns across India
  • Natural Beauty: Stunning waterfalls, plateaus, and valleys

Satara's Natural Treasures:

Being in the Western Ghats, Satara boasts:

  • Kaas Plateau - UNESCO World Heritage Site with rare wildflowers
  • Thoseghar Waterfall - Spectacular 500-foot cascade
  • Rich Agriculture - Fertile soil supporting strawberries, grapes, and sugarcane
  • Pleasant Climate - Year-round moderate temperatures
